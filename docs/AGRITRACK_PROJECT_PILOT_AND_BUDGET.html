1. Executive summary

AgriTrack narrows the trust gap between remote farm owners, farmers, traders, and capital partners by pairing verifiable operational data with transparent financial records, including optional on-chain settlement on the Sui network for dev/test and future production-grade assurance.

The platform combines a web application and NestJS API with Postgres: procurements (“My farm”), a marketplace for listed produce, sales & stock ledgers, mobile-money-aligned UGX bookkeeping (references & payer details), optional Stripe card flows for upgrades and validated marketplace rails where enabled, plus notifications (in-app alerts and SMS activity log). Together, these capabilities turn fragmented field reporting into a single, audit-friendly story from greenhouse to payout.

2. Problem statement & solution

The trust gap Remote owners and investors often cannot independently verify planting, harvest, stock, or how funds moved. Paper processes and disjoint chats invite error, delays, or doubt, tightening cost of capital.
The AgriTrack intervention Digitise listings, trades, credits, and optional on-chain confirmations; surface alerts (low stock, overdue credit) so stakeholders share one operational picture, anchored to your rules and timestamps.

Core capabilities (implemented and pilot-ready)

  • Immutable-oriented records: daily farm logs, procurements and harvests, sales, stored in the central database with audit-friendly history (API).
  • Marketplace & checkout: farmers list; traders purchase; settlements recorded (digital UGX, credit where allowed, optional Sui path for dev).
  • Financial transparency: platform fee and farmer-share views; debt & credit tracking; Stripe for card-backed flows where configured.
  • Remote oversight: dashboards, searches, procurement & sales views, readable by role.
  • Sui Blockchain: optional wallet-linked flows and devnet onboarding path for “pay with Sui” style settlement where farmers publish receiving addresses.

Enhanced pilot lane (hardware and IoT)

Aligning with sensors and CCTV and baseline connectivity (as in your original scope): environmental and visual feeds can complement ledger data, grounding “what happened on the plot” beside “what appears in AgriTrack.” Pilot success pairs sampling frequency and staff discipline with ledger discipline.

3. Pilot implementation strategy

A phased deployment validates end-to-end trust: data capture to listing to transaction to stakeholder review. Suggested runway: up to 6 months active validation (adjust with sponsor).

Phase Activities Outcome
Phase 1: Setup Hosting & API (DATABASE_URL, JWT, email where used); farmer and trader onboarding; optional IoT hardware install (sensors and CCTV). Baseline data & roles established.
Phase 2: Integration Listings live; marketplace activity; Stripe, MoMo, Sui rails as policy allows; SMS log populated; alerts tested. Live ledger + operational transparency.
Phase 3: Review Stakeholder interviews; reconcile stock vs sales; financial spot-checks; backlog for scale. Pilot performance report and go or no-go.

4.7 Budget governance

  • Cap: sponsor approves baseline 11,220,000 UGX or explicitly approves High scenario ~13.7M.
  • Moves: moves over 10% between Hardware, Dev, Ops require written approval.
  • Contingency tap: only with PM and sponsor sign-off; log reason.
  • Evidence: retain invoices and mobile-money receipts for audits.

4.8 Appendix: tech variables that drive spend

  • DATABASE_URL: managed Postgres tier scales with connections and storage.
  • API host tier and build minutes drive traffic and deploy frequency.
  • STRIPE_SECRET_KEY: no licence fee; percentage per successful charge.
  • FRONTEND_URL, email SMTP: transactional mail volume.
  • Sui: devnet negligible; production mainnet adds gas costs and custody policy.

5. Risk management & sustainability

  • Data & uptime: encrypted transport, secrets in environment (Render/Vault), backups on Postgres; runbook for DATABASE_URL and JWT rotation.
  • Blockchain: Sui offers high throughput and predictable costs for on-chain anchors; production path should include key custody policy and network choice (testnet to mainnet governance).
  • Adoption: training, local language support, and incentives aligned with donor rules reduce drop-off.
  • Long-term: modular API allows adding MNO webhooks, IoT feeds, and investor reporting without rebuilding core ledgers.

6. Success criteria (pilot exit)

  • ≥ agreed number of verified listings and recorded trades through AgriTrack.
  • Stock & sales reconciled within agreed tolerance; support volume acceptable.
  • Stakeholder satisfaction ≥ target (survey); no critical security incidents.
  • Clear recommendation: scale, iterate, or pivot, with budget for next phase.
